Padmanabhaswamy Temple

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Padmanabhaswamy Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu located in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, India.

History

The Padmanabhaswamy Temple is an ancient shrine with a rich history dating back to the 8th century AD. It is one of the 108 Divya Desams (holy abodes of Lord Vishnu) mentioned in the works of the Alvars, the revered saint-poets of Tamil Nadu.

Legend: According to Hindu mythology, the Padmanabhaswamy Temple was constructed by Lord Vishnu's incarnation, Lord Parashurama, on the instructions of a divine sage named Vilvamangalathu Swamiyar. The temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, who is worshipped here in the Anantha Shayana posture (reclining position on the serpent Adi Sesha).

Architecture: The temple showcases a blend of Kerala and Dravidian architectural styles. It features a towering gopuram (entrance tower), pillared halls, intricate carvings, and a massive idol of Lord Vishnu.

Significance: The Padmanabhaswamy Temple holds immense religious significance for Hindus, especially for devotees of Lord Vishnu. It is considered one of the holiest temples in India and attracts millions of pilgrims and tourists every year.

Recent Discoveries: In recent years, the temple gained international attention due to the discovery of hidden chambers filled with treasures worth billions of dollars. This unprecedented find has further enhanced the temple's allure and intrigue.

Tourism

Padmanabhaswamy Temple is a renowned tourist destination in Kerala, offering visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural and religious heritage of India. Tourists can explore the temple complex, witness traditional rituals, and experience the spiritual ambiance of this sacred site.
