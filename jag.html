Jagannath Temple

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jagannath Temple is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Jagannath (a form of Lord Vishnu), located in Puri, Odisha, India. It is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites for Hindus and is renowned for its annual Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival).

History

The history of Jagannath Temple dates back to ancient times. According to Hindu mythology, the temple was originally built by King Indradyumna and later renovated by various rulers. It is believed that the present structure of the temple was built in the 12th century by the Ganga dynasty ruler, Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva.

The temple has witnessed several invasions and renovations throughout its history. It has remained a center of pilgrimage and cultural heritage, attracting devotees and tourists from all over the world.

Tourism

Jagannath Temple is not only a sacred pilgrimage site but also a popular tourist destination. Visitors can experience the rich cultural heritage and architectural beauty of the temple complex. The annual Rath Yatra, during which the deities are taken out in grand chariots, is a major attraction for tourists.
