History

The Konark Sun Temple, also known as the Black Pagoda, is a 13th-century Hindu temple dedicated to the sun god Surya. It was built by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty in the shape of a colossal chariot with intricate stone carvings.

Architecture: The temple is renowned for its architectural grandeur and exquisite stone carvings depicting various aspects of Hindu mythology, celestial beings, and daily life scenes. The main sanctum, known as the Natya Mandapa, features a magnificent pyramidal roof adorned with sculptures of lions, elephants, and mythical creatures.

Symbolism: The Konark Sun Temple is symbolic of the sun god's chariot, with twelve pairs of wheels representing the months of the year and seven horses representing the days of the week. The temple's design reflects the cosmic order and the cycle of time.

Decline and Restoration: Over the centuries, the temple fell into disrepair and was partially buried under sand. It was rediscovered and extensively restored in the 20th century, and today, it stands as a testament to India's rich cultural heritage and architectural prowess.

Tourism

The Konark Sun Temple is a major tourist attraction in Odisha, drawing visitors from around the world with its stunning architecture and historical significance. Tourists can explore the temple complex, marvel at the intricate carvings, and learn about the rich cultural heritage of India.
